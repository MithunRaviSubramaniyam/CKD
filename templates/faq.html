What is Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

CKD is a gradual loss of kidney function over time, which can eventually lead to kidney failure if left untreated.

Read More

What are the main causes of CKD

The most common causes of CKD are diabetes and high blood pressure. Other causes include glomerulonephritis and genetic disorders.

Read More

What are the symptoms of CKD

Early-stage CKD often has no symptoms, but as it progresses, you may experience fatigue, swelling, and changes in urination.

Read More

How is CKD diagnosed

CKD is diagnosed through blood tests and urine tests, alongside imaging or kidney biopsy if necessary.

Read More

How can CKD be treated

Treatment focuses on managing the underlying cause and slowing disease progression through medication and lifestyle changes.

Read More

Can CKD be prevented

Yes, controlling diabetes and high blood pressure, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, and regular check-ups can reduce the risk.
